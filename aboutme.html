A few fun facts about myself


I moved to the US when I was 17. I live and work in Boston, MA, but the SF Bay Area is my home. I love to cook and recently started learning how to bake. I'm a huge fan of IPAs. I play the Djembe.

Education

I attended Skyline College, where I completed my A.S. in Natural Sciences in 2012. This is where I got my first introduction to Biomedical Research. While at Skyline, I spent a summer working on a project to test the toxicity of a chemical, Sarcophine, a coral mixture found in the red sea soft coral Sarcophyton glaucum, and study its effects on the growth and development of chicken embryo cells.

After Skyline, I transfered to the University of California Santa Cruz (UCSC), where I obtained my B.S. and graduated with a double major in Chemistry and Molecular, Cell, and Developmental Biology in 2015. At UCSC, I worked in the lab of Dr. Scott Lokey in the Department of Chemistry and Biochemistry. The Lokey lab's main areas of focus are the development of new methodologies for synthesizing “natural product-like” cyclic peptides and understanding the membrane permeability of these compounds and other small molecules.

After my undergraduate studies, I attended Johns Hopkins University and completed my M.Sc. in Bioinformatics in 2018. While pursuing my Masters' degree, I also conducted research in the lab of Dr. Prashant Desai at the Sidney Kimmel Comprehensive Cancer Cener, a branch of the Johns Hopkins University School of Medicine. The research focus of the Desai lab is on the molecular mechanisms of viral species known to cause human diseases, specifically herpesviruses.


Understanding My Passion

There's something to be said about working with passionate scientists that truly love what they do. I’m no different. I started doing research because I honestly enjoy coming to work every day to discover something that has the ability to impact others.
