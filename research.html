My overall goal as a researcher is to help translate our understanding of Cancer Biology and Immunology to develop personalized therapies and diagnostics to transform clinical practice to benefit patient health.

As a multi-disciplinary scientist working at the intersection of Molecular/Cell Biology and Data Science, I am particularly excited and motivated to conduct scientific research and develop computational methods for personalized cancer immunotherapies. To accomplish this, I can be found either at the bench - doing laboratory work, or on the computational front - running bioinformatics analysis.

Laboratory Activities

In the wet lab, I take great pride in conducting innovative research designed to identify and validate immune-based therapies for cancer. From experimental design, to assay development, to protocol optimization, to biological tools generation and instrument maintenance, I use my skills to help advance research platforms and enable my colleagues to do great science.

Bioinformatics Work

My activities on the computational bioloy side provide me with another set of amazing opportunities. First, I get to develop computational tools/software and methods that enable cancer immunotherapy research. I also get to explore and analyze multi-dimensional data (clinical and pre-clinical datasets) as part of the broader efforts to study identify novel targets, and characterize the biological mechanisms of action of novel immunotherapeutic product candidates for the treatment of cancer.
